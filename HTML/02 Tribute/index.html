Here's a time line of his life...

Abdul Qadeer Khan was a Pakistani nuclear physicist and metallurgical engineer. He is colloquially known as the "father of Pakistan's atomic weapons program". In August 2021, Khan was admitted to Khan Research Laboratories Hospital after testing positive for COVID-19. Khan died on 10 October 2021, at the age of 85, after being transferred to a hospital in Islamabad with lung problems. He was given a state funeral at the Faisal Mosque before being buried at the H-8 graveyard in Islamabad.

  • 1 April 1936 — Born in Bhopal, British India
  • 1952 — Migrated to Pakistan, settled in Karachi
  • 1956–1960 — Studied at D.J. Science College / University of Karachi
  • 1961–1967 — Studied metallurgical engineering in Germany and the Netherlands
  • 1967 — Earned Master’s degree from Delft University of Technology
  • 1972 — Completed PhD from Catholic University of Leuven, Belgium
  • 1972–1975 — Worked in the Netherlands at a uranium enrichment facility
  • 1974 — India’s nuclear test; Pakistan accelerates nuclear program
  • 1975 — Returned to Pakistan at PM Zulfikar Ali Bhutto’s request
  • 1976 — Founded Engineering Research Laboratories (ERL)
  • 1981 — ERL renamed Kahuta Research Laboratories (KRL)
  • 1980s–1990s — Led Pakistan’s uranium enrichment & nuclear weapons development
  • 28 May 1998 — Pakistan conducts nuclear tests at Chagai; Khan becomes a national hero
  • 2001 — Retired as Director of KRL
  • 2004 — Public confession and presidential pardon
  • 2010 — Restrictions on movement gradually lifted
  • 10 October 2021 — Passed away in Islamabad, aged 85
